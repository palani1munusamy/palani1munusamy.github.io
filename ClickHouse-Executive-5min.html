Overview

Executive Summary

ClickHouse is an open-source column-oriented OLAP database engineered for sub-second analytical queries over very large datasets — typically 10–1000× faster than traditional row-stores on aggregation workloads.

Fit assessment

When To Use — And When Not To

USE CLICKHOUSE WHEN

  • Analytical aggregations over wide tables
  • Time-series, logs, events, telemetry
  • Append-mostly ingestion at high volume
  • Dashboards needing sub-second latency
  • Datasets too large/expensive for OLTP
  • Real-time observability & SIEM workloads

AVOID CLICKHOUSE WHEN

  • OLTP — many small row-level updates
  • Strict ACID multi-row transactions
  • Heavy joins over large dimensional data
  • Frequent point-lookup or upsert traffic
  • <100M rows where Postgres suffices
  • Unique-constraint enforcement is critical

Positioning

RFP Posture & Operational Readiness

STRENGTHS TO LEAD WITH

  • Cost-to-performance ratio
  • Open source — no licensing or vendor lock-in
  • Real-time ingest with sub-second query latency
  • Linear horizontal scaling on commodity infrastructure

RISKS TO ACKNOWLEDGE

  • Operational expertise is non-trivial — invest in SRE skills
  • Schema design heavily shapes performance
  • Limited transactional guarantees; updates are expensive
  • JOIN ergonomics weaker than purpose-built DWs
RECOMMENDATION FOR SRE / PLATFORM TEAMS · Treat ClickHouse as a first-class platform service: dedicated capacity plan, observability via system tables, defined HA/DR runbooks, RBAC integration, and a clear ingest contract. Pilot one observability or product-analytics workload before committing to broader migration. Use ClickHouse Cloud for initial deployment unless data-residency or cost at scale dictates self-managed Kubernetes operator.
